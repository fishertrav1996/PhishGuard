What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals send fraudulent emails that appear to come from legitimate sources. The goal is to trick you into clicking malicious links, downloading harmful attachments, or revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers.

How to Spot Phishing Emails

  • Check the sender's email address - Hover over the sender name to see the actual email address. Phishers often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones.
  • Look for urgency or threats - Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency ("Your account will be suspended!") to pressure you into acting quickly.
  • Hover over links before clicking - Before clicking any link, hover your mouse over it to see the actual URL. Does it match the supposed sender?
  • Watch for spelling and grammar errors - Professional organizations proofread their emails. Multiple errors can be a red flag.
  • Be wary of unexpected attachments - Don't open attachments from unknown senders or unexpected emails.
  • Verify requests for sensitive information - Legitimate companies won't ask for passwords or sensitive data via email.

What Should You Do?

If you receive a suspicious email:

  • Don't click any links or download attachments
  • Report it to your IT or security team
  • Delete the email
  • If you've already clicked, change your passwords immediately and notify IT
